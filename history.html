During the 18th century, a family from the place of Vellankalloor near Iringalakkuda of Thrissur district came, first at Edappally, then at Edamattam and then to Kothala. It is believed that the family name Vellakkallumkal came from Vellankalloor. Then the matriarchy system was prevalent and the family was ruled by women. The orthodox Nair family at that time had a practice of worshipping Gandharvan for the well being of the members of the family. An elderly woman of this family, who was a spinster and an ardent worshipper of Gandharva had got the ‘darshan’ of Gandharva and he promised that he would protect all the coming generations of the family. And for that Gandharva, the temple was built and an ancestor who made the rules for poojas was also worshipped as Yogeeshvaran in the temple. The Gandharvaswami is believed to be the reincarnation of Kamadeva as the son of Sree Krishna and Rugmini.
